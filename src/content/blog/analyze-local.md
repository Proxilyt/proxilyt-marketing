If your competitors consistently outrank you, they’re ranking for something you’re not.

Here’s how to analyze local competitor keywords the right way.

---

## Step 1: Identify Your True Local Competitors

Your business competitors and SEO competitors aren’t always the same.

Search your primary service keywords in Google and note:

- Who ranks in the top 3 organic spots
- Who appears in the local map pack
- Who consistently shows up across related queries

Those are your SEO competitors.

---

## Step 2: Analyze Keyword Overlap

Next, compare:

- Keywords you rank for
- Keywords competitors rank for
- Keywords they rank for that you don’t

This reveals “keyword gaps.”

These gaps often represent:

- High-intent local searches
- Service variations you haven’t targeted
- Long-tail opportunities

---

## Step 3: Evaluate Search Intent

Not all keywords are equal.

Look for:

- Transactional intent (hire, near me, best, service)
- Commercial investigation keywords
- Location modifiers

Prioritize keywords that drive revenue, not just traffic.

---

## Step 4: Review Content Depth

If a competitor outranks you, compare:

- Word count
- Page structure
- Headings
- FAQs
- Internal links
- Backlinks

Often the difference is structural, not just keyword usage.

---

## Step 5: Turn Data Into Action

Competitive keyword research should lead to:

- New service pages
- Optimized landing pages
- Localized content
- FAQ additions
- Internal linking improvements

Data without execution does nothing.
